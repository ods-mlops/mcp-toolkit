"We met with the regional operations team last Tuesday and it's clear there are some serious alignment issues. The VP of Supply Chain mentioned that inventory levels at the Dallas facility are running about 40% above target, which is tying up roughly $2.3 million in working capital. The warehouse manager said the root cause is a forecasting model that hasn't been updated since 2021 and doesn't account for the new product lines we added last year. On top of that, the ERP system upgrade that was supposed to go live in Q1 has been pushed to Q3 due to budget constraints, which means the team is still running manual reconciliation processes three times a week. Sarah from finance flagged that carrying costs are eating into the margin on the new product lines and if nothing changes by end of year we'll miss our EBITDA target by approximately 8%. The operations team wants to pilot a new demand planning tool but nobody has ownership of the decision and the IT team hasn't been looped in yet. There's also tension between the warehouse manager and the procurement lead over who is responsible for safety stock levels."
